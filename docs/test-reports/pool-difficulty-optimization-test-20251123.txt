===============================================================================
                Pool Difficulty Optimization Test Report
===============================================================================

**Date**: 2025-11-23 13:06–13:12  
**Goal**: Determine whether increasing pool difficulty lowers the block rejection rate.

---

## 1. Configuration

Baseline:
- `pool.initialDifficulty = 1000`
- `miner.nonceRange = 10000`
- `miner.threads = 2`

Optimized:
- `pool.initialDifficulty = 50000` (50× higher)
- Miner settings unchanged

---

## 2. Results

| Metric                | Baseline (1000) | Optimized (50000) | Delta |
|-----------------------|-----------------|-------------------|-------|
| Accepted blocks       | 43              | 19                | -56%  |
| Rejected blocks       | 150             | 81                | -46%  |
| Rejection rate        | 77.6%           | 81.0%             | +3.4% |
| Share submission rate | ~50–100/s       | ~50/s             | -50%  |
| Pool CPU usage        | 191%            | 179%              | -6%   |

Conclusion: **Pool difficulty alone does not reduce the rejection rate.**

---

## 3. Root cause

`nonceRange = 10000` forces the miner to request new work after 10k nonces regardless of whether a
valid share is found. As a result:
- Miner completes 10k hashes every 0.02s and requests new work.
- Submission frequency stays fixed (~50 per second) regardless of pool difficulty.
- Shares arrive in bursts and most become stale after the candidate changes.

The real culprit is **candidate competition**:
1. Pool builds candidate A.
2. Miner finds multiple nonces that meet block difficulty.
3. First accepted share updates the candidate to B.
4. Remaining shares still reference A → rejected.
5. Additional blocks from other nodes cause more candidate switches → even more rejections.

Mathematically, if 10 valid shares appear within an epoch, 1 is accepted and 9 are rejected,
yielding ~90% rejection rate independent of pool difficulty.

---

## 4. Observations

- Increasing pool difficulty reduces share submission frequency (100/s → 50/s) and lowers pool CPU
  usage slightly, but rejection rate remains high because shares still target outdated candidates.
- Two-node competition accelerates candidate rotation (Suite1 + Suite2 both producing blocks).

---

## 5. Next steps

**Option A – increase `nonceRange` (recommended)**  
`nonceRange = 10000 → 1000000` would:
- Reduce submission rate from ~50/s to ~0.5/s.
- Allow miners to keep working on the same candidate longer, drastically reducing stale shares.

**Option B – adaptive pool difficulty**  
Add logic to raise pool difficulty dynamically when share frequency exceeds a safe threshold, but
this must be combined with a larger `nonceRange` to be effective.
