===============================================================================
          77.6% Block Rejection Rate – Root Cause Analysis Report
===============================================================================

Date: 2025-11-23  
Environment: two-node devnet with full mining workload

---

## Problem statement

Observation:
```
Accepted blocks : 43
Rejected blocks : 150
Rejection rate  : 77.6%
```
User concern: “Is this rejection rate abnormal? Is something broken?”

---

## Findings

1. **Pool configuration**
   - `initialDifficulty = 1000` → the core issue.
   - Real block difficulty during the run: 109–342.
   - Pool difficulty was far too low.

2. **Miner behavior**
   - Submission frequency: 25–100 shares/second.
   - Total shares per 64-second epoch: roughly 1,600–6,400.
   - Extremely high submission volume.

3. **Candidate block mechanics**
   - Pool requests a new candidate every 64 seconds.
   - Once a block is accepted, the candidate immediately changes.
   - Any share produced against the old candidate is rejected.

---

## Timeline example (single 64-second epoch)

```
T=0s   Pool fetches Candidate A (epoch 100)
T=0-64s Miner submits thousands of shares; ~10 satisfy block difficulty

T=10s  Share #1 matches target → ACCEPTED (Candidate switches to B)
T=12s  Share #2 also matches but still references Candidate A → REJECTED
T=15s  Suite2 broadcasts a block → ACCEPTED (Candidate switches to C)
T=18s  Share #3 references Candidate A → REJECTED
...
```

In this epoch, 1 share is accepted while ~9 are rejected → ~90% rejection rate.

---

## Mathematical model

Let:
- `N` = total shares submitted in 64 seconds ≈ 2,000
- `M` = shares that meet block difficulty ≈ 10
- `R` = blocks produced by the peer node ≈ 2–3

Process:
1. The first valid share wins and updates the candidate.
2. Remaining `M-1` shares were mined on the old candidate and are rejected.
3. Additional remote blocks (`R`) may update the candidate again, further invalidating pending shares.

Result:
- Accepted = 1 (sometimes 2–3 if lucky)
- Rejected = `M - accepted` ≈ 7–9
- Rejection rate = 70–90% → matches the observed **77.6%**

---

## Conclusion

- ❌ Not a bug.
- ✅ Misconfiguration: pool difficulty set too low.

Consequences:
- Flood of share submissions.
- Majority of shares become stale before being submitted.
- Pool ↔ node communication wastes CPU/bandwidth but does not affect consensus correctness.

Positive side effect: the test confirms the system remains stable under extreme share pressure
(25–100 share submissions per second, 3–4 blocks per minute).
